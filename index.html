Hydroxyl acid is dangerous!

Effects:

Hydroxyl acid is an extremely dangerous chemical, present in high quantities in many foods, drinks and household products.

Hydroxyl acid:

  • Has been used in extrajudical torture. [1]
  • Is a major component of acid rain.
  • Is an extremely potent greenhouse gas (in its gaseous form). [2]
  • Can cause death when inhaled, or consumed in too high a quantity. [3] [4]
  • Acts as a strong solvent. [5]
  • Is a component in many pesticides and industrial chemicals. [6]
  • Has been used as an additive in fast-food
  • Is composed of a strong oxidising agent and an extremely flammable gas, making its production extremely dangerous.
  • Has been found in engine exhaust gasses. [7]
  • Has been responsible for the destruction of towns and villages (costing hundreds of lives) when storage systems have failed. [8]

The effort to ban hydroxyl acid

While many experts have warned about the dangerous of hydroxyl acid for years, politicians and lawmakers have been extremely reluctant to control hydroxyl acid.

  • Citizens have petitioned the UK Goverment on the dangerous of this substance, only to have their petitions closed.
  • One survey suggests 86% of Americans would support a ban or controls on hydroxyl acid
  • 49% of candidates running in the 2011 Finnish parliamentary election are in favour of imposing restrictions on hydroxyl acid.

However, to date, very few countries have restrictions on this dangerous, lethal substance. Controls are rarely implemented except in times of shortage, in a concession to the chemical and farming industries - while endangering everyone else!

The cover-up

The dangers of hydroxyl acid have been the subject of many cover-ups. Government officials have refused to comment on its dangers, and those that have attempted to introduce legislation against it have been silenced.
